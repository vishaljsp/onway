COURSE OVERVIEW

MBBS stands for Bachelor of Medicine and Bachelor of Surgery. It is a Degree dual in Nature Conferred by the Medical University Approved and Recognized by NATIONAL MEDICAL COMMISSION. Earlier the Degree was Recognized by MCI but from 25th Sep 2020 It is being replaced by NMC i.e NATIONAL MEDICAL COMMISSION. The Duration of this Course is 5.5 Yrs including 1 year of Internship in Various Medical Camps, Health Centers and Community Hubs etc. Sometimes It might be paid. India has been the Most Favoured Nation to Pursue MBBS in the Entire World because of its Versatile Clinical Exposure and for One of the Best Medical Practices.

Indian Degrees are Widely Recognized by Most of the International Establishments like WHO, UNESCO, and FAIMER, etc. Indian Doctors are Well Known for the best Understanding of Critical Diseases and being India is a Highly Populated Region, The Clinical Exposure is the Maximum in Comparison to any Asian or European Country.

 MBBS course syllabus includes studies on anatomy, pharmacology, pathology as well as community health & medicine, pediatrics, and surgery. In the syllabus, MBBS degree holders can choose a specialization for further majoring and practicing medicine. The career options for MBBS students are Nephrology, Cardiology, Gynecology, Anesthesiology, Organ Transplant, Endocrine, and General Surgery, etc.

Course Highlights of MBBS

  • Course:  MBBS (Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery)
  • Course Duration: 5.5 Years + 1 Year of Internship
  • Eligibility: 50% or above in 10+2 in PCB with English as a mandatory subject
  • Entrance Exam: NEET (The National Eligibility cum Entrance Test)

MBBS Admission Process:

 

In order to get admission to a Medical College in India, One should pass the NEET exam Conducted by NTA (NATIONAL TESTING AGENCY). There is a certain process for Occupying a seat in a Medical College to fulfill One’s Aspiration.

  • Registration is the very first process for the Entrance exam in which basic details like Candidate Name, Date of Birth, Mobile Number, E-mail Id are to be filled.
  • After Registration, The form filling Process starts in which All the Details Including Personal and Academic Details are to be filled along with the Uploading of all the Documents Required.
  • After successfully filling the form the Candidate has to pay the Application Fee.
  • The next step is to click on the ‘Confirmed’ Tab as It is necessary to take out the Print with all the Information Provided by the Candidate himself/herself.
  • On the basis of Details fed by the Candidate the Admit Card is Issued which is Quite Necessary to Attempt the Exam.
  • After attempting the exam, Results are Published in which the Candidate must qualify with a Good Score to be in the Merit List because This list will determine the College that the Candidate is going to be Offered.

On the ground of Score One gets in the Entrance Exam, Candidates are called for the Counselling where they need to Select the College from the list of Colleges which will be Offered to them On the Basis of Rank they Achieved. At this Stage of Admission, a Candidate should take the help of an Experienced Counselor. Counseling is a very arduous task and it must be Well Articulated.

Advantage of Studying MBBS in India

Recognition of Degree :

Degrees conferred to the passing out Medical Students by MCI Approved Medical Colleges are Eligible to Practice Anywhere Across the Globe because of the Worldwide Acceptance of Degrees.

Affordable Fee Structure  :

On the basis of NEET Score If a Candidate scores high then He/She can Opt for a Medical College where the Course Fees is as Less as Negligible. Many Private Colleges are also Affordable to Pursue MBBS.

 

Accommodation and Food  :

Almost Every Medical College in India facilitates Good Hostel with Canteens for a very Decent Charge so it has never been a Concern for any Medical Student within the Country.

Clinical Practice :

Being the 2nd Highly Populated Country in the World, Indian Hospitals are Offered Very Much Exposure to a Variety of Disease which Enables a Medical Student to get the Best Clinical Practice.

Advancement of Medical research :

Indian Government is putting Its Best Effort to bring the Medical Advancement to a new level, So a New Medical Commission is formed in Order to Produce World-Class Doctors and for Setting a New Benchmark for the Advance Research as Well.

World-class Infrastructure:

Hundreds of Private Medical Colleges in India are Provided with World Class Infrastructure, Large Campus Area, State of the Art Labs, and All the Necessary Components to Produce a Competent Doctor.
