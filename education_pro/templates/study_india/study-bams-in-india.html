ABOUT AYURVEDA AND ITS RELEVANCE

The Word ‘AYURVEDA’ is a combination of two terms ‘AYUR’ which means ‘LIFE’ and ‘VEDA’ stands for ‘KNOWLEDGE’. This is the Oldest Health Science which was Originated More than 5000 Years ago. Its roots go to ‘Vedic Texts’ and It was taught for Thousands of Years by the Accomplished Masters to their Disciples. The Principal of many Natural Treatment Systems in the West found Its Origin in ‘AYURVEDA’ like ‘HOMEOPATHY’ and ‘POLARITY THERAPY’. Knowledge of Ayurveda makes one have the Best Understanding of the Function of the Human Body and Everything about the Balance of the Body. 

It also describes Human Anatomy in a very Spectacular manner. For its Magical Effects, It is also Considered as’ Mother Of All Healing’. In short ‘AYURVEDA’ is the greatest gift for the ‘humankind’ and it has successfully established itself as a boon for the entire human species.

Because of its Usefulness and A Well Defined Combination of Modern Medicine, The Ministry of Ayush, A functional Ministry of Indian Government Constitutes Many UG and PG courses Which have been Quite Popular for the Last Decade. Hundreds of Ayurvedic Colleges have been Opened in the last few years and There are also many PRIVATE COLLEGES that are being set up in Almost Every State of the Country. Across the length and breadth of this Country, Hundreds of Ayurvedic Colleges are there.

ABOUT BAMS:

‘BAMS’ stands for Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery. This is an Undergraduate Degree Program Approved and Well Recognized By None Other than the Ministry of Ayush, Government of India. Areas of study in Ayurveda are as follows.

 

  • Current Life Structure
  • Human Anatomy
  • Social and Preventive Pharmaceuticals
  • Toxicology
  • Physiology
  • General Medicine
  • Herbal Science
  • Para Surgery.

SANSKRIT is taught in the Entire Course for a Better Understanding of the Subjects. This Degree Course is a Very Well Defined Blend of both Ancient Health Science and Modern Advanced Medicine. Students can also Add Value to their Study and Knowledge by Combining Therapies such as PANCHAKARMA, PHYSIOTHERAPY, DIETETICS, and YOGA. This is a well Established Treatment System and Millions of People Across the Globe are being Benefited from this Blend of Traditional Ayurveda and Modern Medicine. Patients come across the Globe for Ayurvedic Treatment so as the Popularity is going up, The Demand for Professional Ayurvedic Practitioner is also Soaring Up with the Same Pace. So in any means, Ayurvedic is None less than Modern Medicine.

Course Duration & Eligibility:

The entire duration of the Course of ‘Bachelor of Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery’ is 5.5 Years In which One Year of Internship is Mandatory. It can be accomplished in Health Camps, Wellness Centers, Ayurvedic Hospitals and also In Affiliated Private Ayurvedic Hospitals.

  • Students should pass 10+2 with 50% Marks from the Recognized Board.
  • Physics, Chemistry, and Biology are the Compulsory Subjects in 12th Board.
  • Valid Score Card

Admission Process in BAMS 2020:

Admission in Any Ayurvedic Medical College to Pursue ‘BAMS’ is Completely based on the Score obtained in NEET followed by the Counseling. Once a candidate is qualified in the ‘NEET’ he/she will be able to take part in the Centralized Counseling Process where College will be Assigned on the basis of Performance in the ‘NEET’. Some Colleges may take Personal Interviews of the Candidate before Allotting Seats to them.

Career Aspects of BAMS :

A few years back there were limited opportunities for a Graduate in BAMS but now it has many Promising Scopes to lead a Successful Career in different fields. The Ministry of Ayush designed many Postgraduate Courses for the Graduates in Ayurvedic Medicine and Surgery. After completion of these courses, One can easily pursue a bright career in the field of Ayurvedic Medicine. Some of the Broad Prospects of Career after BAMS are as follows :

Clinical Practice

Option for Clinical Practice is always Open because of its Popularity which has been acquiring great heights day by day. For the last few years, many Foreign Countries have come to know about the Importance of AYURVEDA. Every year thousands of people from Different Countries come to India to receive Ayurvedic Treatment and Due to its Effectiveness and Zero Side Effect, it is being quite popular across the Globe so the Clinical Practice is always a great option for a BAMS graduate. One can also add value to their Knowledge and Study by Pursuing Postgraduate Courses in AYURVEDA like MD IN AYURVEDA or MS IN AYURVEDA. Along with these courses, there are also many Diploma and Certificate Courses in AYURVEDA. A Postgraduate Diploma in EMERGENCY MEDICINE is a 6 Month Course offered by Hinduja hospital, Mumbai or Ruby Clinic in Pune can add value to the Clinical Practice of a BAMS student. There are also many therapies which is a sort of Specialization in

  • PANCHAKARMA
  • PHYSIOTHERAPY
  • THERAPY OF SKIN DISEASE
  • SPINAL DISORDER
  • OPHTHALMOLOGIC DISORDERS
  • And Many more Such Therapies where AYURVEDA has a special role to play.

Academics

A student after completing Post Graduation in AYURVEDIC MEDICINE can certainly Opt for a Career as an Academician. Hundreds of Private and Government Colleges are in the need of Demonstrators and Lecturers. Pursue a Career in Academics has been always in Demand especially in the field of AYURVEDIC MEDICINE. Recently there are many M.Sc Programs offered by Different Universities in Physiology, Anatomy, Microbiology for BAMS students. The Successful Completion of these courses will help you to build a Successful Career in Academics.

Some of the Universities where M.Sc Course is being offered to BAMS Students are as follows :

Research

There are vast options for Research in AYURVEDA. There are many good Courses offered for the Purpose of Research In AYURVEDA even Many colleges abroad offer Fellowship for Different Streams of Research in AYURVEDA. Postgraduate Diploma in Clinical Research is a Course Intently designed for the sake of Clinical Research. These Courses are available in Various Institutions and Universities throughout the Country.
CRANFIELD UNIVERSITY OF UK also Offers a Masters Degree in Clinical Research through its Branches in India. These courses enable one to make a Successful Career as a Research Associate in a Pharmaceutical Company. A Student can also join as a Junior Research fellow in many Research Projects conducted by many Institutions and Universities Financially Supported by ICMR, CSIR, CCRAS, etc. In short, the field of Research is always Promising for a BAMS Student because of the Popularity of AYURVEDA

Drug Manufacturing

Drug Manufacturing is a Booming Sector because of the High Demand for Herbal Medicine and so as Ayurvedic Practitioners too. There is hunting going on for the Ayurvedic Graduates In order to fulfill the demands of Herbal Medicine which require a Vast Knowledge of Medicinal Plants, Extraction of Raw Material in the form of Powder, Oil, etc. Not only Ayurvedic Medicines but the Cosmetics and Food Market is also growing at the Same Pace so there is a Huge Demand for the Ayurvedic Graduates In Manufacturing Sector.

One can also groom his Career by Pursuing some Need of the hour Courses like B.Pharm (AYURVEDA) in Jamnagar, MBA in Medicinal Marketing, M.Sc in Pharmaceutical Medicine, Diploma in Herbal Medicinal manufacturing which is Conducted by Pune University.

 

Scope & Future of Ayurveda

A career in Ayurveda is good, as more people are turning to Ayurvedic therapy. Ayurveda is one of the oldest medical therapies currently available to study and practice. An Ayurvedic practitioner can get employment as a medical officer or a doctor at government and private Ayurvedic hospitals. In the age of startups and small businesses, there is good scope for own practice and open a clinic for ayurvedic therapies.

If you like teaching and are passionate about educating people about Ayurveda then there is a satisfactory career in teaching as well. The teaching field is also open for BAMS graduates.

Career after BAMS

After the completion of the BAMS degree, one can either continue general practice or pursue higher studies (MD (Ayurveda)) & MS (Ayurveda) post-graduate diplomas in various subjects such as Panchakarma, Balaroga, etc. or pursue research programs PhD. There are also opportunities to work in hospital and healthcare administration and health supervision.
